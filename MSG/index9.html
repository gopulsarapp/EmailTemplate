What Happens During a Cyberattack

Cyberattacks don’t take days to cause damage. In many cases, the first 30 minutes decide everything.

The difference between a minor incident and a full business outage often comes down to how quickly the attack is detected.

A Typical Cyberattack Timeline

Minute 0 Initial breach via phishing email or stolen credentials.
Minute 5 Attacker establishes persistence and explores the network.
Minute 15 Privilege escalation begins; security gaps are exploited.
Minute 30 With monitoring: Threat detected and contained.
Hour 2 Without monitoring: Data exfiltration or ransomware deployment.
Day 1+ Business outage, recovery costs, legal exposure, and reputational damage.

Attackers move fast. If detection takes hours—or days—the damage is already done.

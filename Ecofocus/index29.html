Why Universities Are Slow to Adopt ESG Reporting

By ABCD
ABCD University

ESG reporting is fast becoming a measure of institutional credibility — yet higher education continues to lag behind other sectors.

Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) reporting has emerged as a critical benchmark for transparency. However, fragmented governance, siloed data, and competing academic priorities slow progress.

Structural and operational barriers to ESG reporting in universities

This hesitation represents a missed opportunity. Universities influence public policy, manage large-scale infrastructure, and shape future leaders. Structured ESG reporting aligns purpose with measurable outcomes.

As expectations around climate accountability rise, ESG reporting is no longer optional — it is foundational to long-term resilience.
